Commencement Poem

In fields of earth, beneath the sky so wide,
Potatoes sleep, where sun and rain abide.
Beneath the soil, they quietly grow,
In darkness, hidden from the world's bright glow.

From humble eyes, they sprout with tender care,
Emerging from the earth, a sight so rare.
Their skin, a coat of brown, rough and plain,
Yet within lies magic, a culinary domain.

From mashed to fried, in countless forms they shine,
A versatile gem, in every dish they intertwine.
Boiled or roasted, their flavors bloom,
In soups and salads, they chase away gloom.

Potatoes, humble spuds, so simple yet profound,
In every kitchen, their presence is found.
A staple of meals, from day to night,
Potatoes, oh potatoes, a comforting delight.

So let us raise a fork, a toast to thee,
Potatoes, the unsung hero, for all to see.
In every bite, a taste of home we find,
In potatoes, the humble, we leave our worries behind.
